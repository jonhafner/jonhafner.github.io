To calculate what travel fee may be necessary, I first use Google Maps to determine the distance of the round trip to our meeting location from my home or office, depending on where I’ll usually be departing from or returning to based on our schedule. (I live just south of Akron in the Portage Lakes area, and I work on the main UAkron campus.) I then multiply that distance by the IRS standard mileage rate for the current year. (For example, for 2019, that’s 58¢/mile.) I round down to the nearest multiple of $5, then absorb the first $5 of that cost myself. Any excess, I must pass along to the client as a necessary premium to cover the cost of my transportation.

I realize that my method may sound somewhat esoteric or cumbersome. When negotiating locations and schedules, I will calculate the relevant travel fees and share that information with you in advance, as I understand that it may impact your decisions. I have no intent to smuggle in hidden fees!

Location: With new clients, I usually conduct sessions in a public place, such as a library, café, or fast food restaurant. (Some students prefer quiet and reserve study rooms in libraries, while others like a bit of background noise or the option to get a bit to eat or drink. I have little preference myself.) In-home tutoring may also be an option once we all get to know each other a bit, or if we connect through a personal referral.

Scheduling: Inquire for availability. I’m generally pretty flexible with scheduling and rescheduling, within the bounds of my availability.
