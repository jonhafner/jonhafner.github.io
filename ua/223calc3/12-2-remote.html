A correction: Near the end of the video, when I used travel on Earth as an example, four-years-ago me made a blunder when speaking off the cuff. For it to work correctly, the distance traveled needs to take us down to the equator; otherwise, the travel east would not be a geodesic (a locally “straight” path). Just expand the lengths correspondingly, and the basic idea holds.

Afterward, you’ll still want to read the rest of this subsection in the textbook.

In words instead of symbols, the Properties of Vectors in the box midway through this subsection tell us that vector addition and scalar multiplication are associative, commutative, and distributive, 0 is the additive identity, and 1 is the multiplicative identity. This is analogous to the algebra of real numbers.
