§ 16.9: The Divergence Theorem

This is also a short section, and the Divergence Theorem is usually relatively simple to apply. It basically tells us that if a field expands or contracts throughout a region, it must have a net flux across its boundary; this should be clear if you think about the movement of a fluid, for instance. Read the text, and I work through an example in “§ 16.9: Divergence Theorem example” (YouTube | Panopto).
