§ 13.4: Motion in Space: Velocity and Acceleration

Velocity, Speed, and Acceleration

Read this subsection carefully. I mentioned in the notes for § 13.2 that the tangent vector can be thought of as velocity; this idea that the first derivative is velocity and the second is acceleration is a natural extension of what you did in Calculus I. I work through an example in “§ 13.4a: Velocity, acceleration, and speed (Exercise #12)” (YouTube | Panopto).

Tangential and Normal Components of Acceleration

This decomposition of acceleration is an important idea, but not a major topic. Read this subsection carefully, but don’t worry about memorizing these formulae; feel free to reference them to answer the exercises about them. I don’t recall ever seeing this topic appear on a final exam.

The example of a passenger in a car is a good way to understand the normal component of acceleration. Continuing with that, the tangential component is parallel to the direction of travel; this translates to the forward or backward force you feel when the driver presses the gas or brake pedals.
