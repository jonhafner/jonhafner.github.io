Calculus III

§ 14.1: Functions of Several Variables

Read this section carefully. The examples in the text are quite thorough, and the illustrations are often superior to what I can produce by hand. (If you aren’t interested in economics and/or find the examples dealing with the Cobb-Douglas function to be difficult to understand, feel free to skim or skip those.)

If you have a solid understanding of functions of a single variable, including their domains and ranges, you should find most of this material to be a natural extension of those concepts. If you struggle a bit with those ideas, however, you may need to take more care. In particular, if you’re in the habit of thinking, “domain is x-values, range is y-values,” you will need to refine your understanding, as that no longer holds. (It doesn’t even hold for functions of a single variable, as when a position x is a function of time t, for instance.)

If you would like to see me work through an analysis of a function, I do so in “§ 14.1: A function of two variables” (YouTube | Panopto).
