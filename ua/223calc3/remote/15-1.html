Calculus III

§ 15.1: Double Integrals over Rectangles

There’s quite a bit in this section, but it’s generally explained thoroughly, I feel; read it carefully. I have little to add, other than to encourage you to try to keep the Riemann sum definitions of integrals in mind. The details and notation are less important than the general idea that, intuitively speaking, integrals can be thought of much like sums, with differentials being sort of like very small deltas. That perspective can help with geometric and physical interpretations throughout this chapter and the next. I work through some examples here:
