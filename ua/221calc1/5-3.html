§ 5.3: The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

This astonishingly powerful theorem lives up to its grandiose name. Read this section carefully! I work through several examples in § 5.3a: FTC1 examples and § 5.3b: FTC2 examples.

There’s a brief discussion of notation in the text between Examples 5 and 6. In my experience, the “other common notations” are more common than the one Stewart primarily uses. Personally, I feel that the lone right bracket ] feels unbalanced. The version with the vertical bar | is a natural extension of other uses of that symbol, and it’s quick and easy to write, so I tend to favor it when it applies to an expression with only a single term. When the function being evaluated has more than one term, I tend to use the paired brackets […], so as to properly enclose the whole thing. However, this is just a set of personal preferences; you are certainly free to use Stewart’s notation if you prefer.
