§ 5.5: The Substitution Rule

The substitution rule, also called a “change of variable”, is essentially a reversal of the Chain Rule. It tends to be particularly useful when we wish to integrate something that involves a composition of functions, though it has other applications as well. This is a very powerful technique, but it’s also a bit tricky and subtle, and it can take some practice. Integration is sometimes a bit of an art.
