Calculus III

§ 16.1*: Vector Fields

Handout

This section is mostly unmodified. However, to fill out a bit more on the topic of conservative vector fields, and to prepare us for some of the material in § 16.5*, I’m bringing in a little bit of the content of § 16.3, regarding identifying whether a two-dimensional vector field is conservative, and if so, how to find its potential function. I will present the essentials in class, and you can find more information on pp. 1090–2 in the textbook. (For now, ignore all the bits about line integrals.)

You may wish to practice a few of those problems before tackling the three-dimensional case in § 16.5*. Exercises #3–10 in § 16.3 most directly pertain to this. In WebAssign, the relevant problems are #3–5 on the homework and #2 on the quiz.
