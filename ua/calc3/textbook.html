Calculus III

Textbook and WebAssign

The textbook will be your primary resource for this course. It’s packed with useful information, illustrations, examples, and references. I will post notes to help guide you through the readings, and the bulk of my lectures will be designed to supplement (with additional examples) or complement (with alternate viewpoints) the textbook; I cannot replace it. I myself learned calculus from 3rd edition Stewart, and its modern descendants are even more thorough and extensive. This text isn’t perfect, but it is the best one I’ve seen. (Or at least, its content is, as is its printed form; I readily admit that the presentation of the online eBook version is … not optimal. See below for other options.)

WebAssign

The eBook is only one portion of a broader system called WebAssign, which includes a variety of assessment tools and other resources. This semester, I am not attaching any sort of points or grades to anything in WebAssign; it is a strictly optional resource. Feel free to use it as much or as little as you please. I have activated the premade “assignments” that were provided by the publisher, if you find such things helpful. (I must stress that those were put together by Cengage, not by myself or anyone else at UAkron. If a question seems particularly strange to you, feel free to check with me to confirm how relevant it is to our course.) In addition to those assessments, it also has an extensive library of videos and other resources.

Opting out of Inclusive Access

There are a couple situations in which you might want to opt out of this arrangement to recover the fee charged to you. First and foremost, if you already have access through some other means, such as a Cengage Unlimited subscription, you should probably opt out of this program to avoid paying extra.

You might also wish to opt out if you already have a copy of the textbook or are able to find one at a good price, and you are not particularly interested in the eBook’s video examples and the other resources WebAssign offers. See below for my recommendations on which editions of the textbook will serve your needs.

If you do wish to opt out, see the statement in the syllabus and the referenced website for more information.

Textbook Versions

The assigned textbook for this course is Calculus: Early Transcendentals, 9th Edition, by James Stewart et al., published by Cengage Learning. A digital “eBook” version is available through WebAssign, as described above, but if your only access is digital, I would personally recommend picking up a physical copy of the text for convenience. (The print version also has superior layout and typography and fewer errors.)

If you wish to seek a cheaper used copy, the standard (i.e., non–Early Transcendentals) version of the text should also work for our class, as should Stewart’s Multivariable Calculus, though in both cases, some minor details may differ, so proceed with caution. Earlier editions should also work, though the further back you go, the more “drift” there will be in exercises, section numbers, and other details. Still, most of the content is the same. Do not buy Single Variable Calculus, as that text explicitly excludes the material of this course.
