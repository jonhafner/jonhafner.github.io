§ 15.7: Triple Integrals in Cylindrical Coördinates

Cylindrical is basically just polar with a z-axis slapped on—or equivalently, ℝ³ except using polar for the horizontal directions. With that in mind, the text (and the video in the eBook) should help fill in the details; pay special attention to the figures illustrating the geometry. I work through an example combining most of the ideas of this section in “§ 15.7: Converting an integral to cylindrical (Exercise #32)” (YouTube | Panopto).
