§ 8.4*: Modular Arithmetic with Applications to Cryptography

Suggested Reading

Be sure to read the section introduction, as well as the second subsection, “Modular Arithmetic”. (We covered the first subsection in § 8.3½.) That covers the required material; I encourage you to read as much of the rest of the section as interests you. (You will need the mathematical theory if you want to really dig into RSA cryptography.)

Exercises

The new material (building on what I mentioned for § 8.3½) is covered in #7–18. #12 and 13 are probably the most broadly useful, but as far as what you might expect on tests, that would be questions of the basic type of #14–18, except of course with much smaller numbers so they can be easily done without a calculator. (These exercises expect you to use a calculator to assist with the arithmetic. Even with the calculator, remember to use the techniques of this section: so, for example, in #14, you should not compute 1416 directly.)

The rest of the exercises relate to the optional portions of this section. Do those which are relevant to your interests.
