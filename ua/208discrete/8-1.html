§ 8.1: Relations on Sets

Suggested Reading

Entire section. Pay particular attention to Example 8.1.2: the concept of congruence will be important later. As mentioned in class, I won’t require you to draw directed graphs, but you may find them to be useful tools. I also won’t ask you anything about database syntax, but you should still read that portion to get the main ideas.

Exercises

#2 is a nice throwback to Chapter 4; as we approach the final, it could be useful to make sure you can still write proofs of that type. The fact stated also has relevance to the important congruence exercises, #1 and #3. Pay close attention to all three of these examples.

After that, do enough of #4–11 to be confident that you can work with anything of this type. (If you don’t work through all of them, at least read them all to see more examples of the variety of types of relations.)

In class, I alluded to the issues addressed by #12, but not in great detail; make sure you understand this exercise thoroughly.

The remaining exercises, #13–24, may be useful for additional practice if you’ve exhausted the other exercises available, but you won’t be explicitly tested on any of those tasks.
