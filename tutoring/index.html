Jon Hafner’s Tutoring Info

Qualifications and Experience: I have tutored in some capacity off and on for roughly two decades. In recent years, my primary employment has been as a part-time instructor in the Department of Mathematics at The University of Akron, from which I received my M.S. (Master of Science degree) in 2012. I also taught at the Western Campus of the Cuyahoga Community College for a year. Previous to that, I worked for four years primarily as a tutor for the ACT, SAT, and other standardized exams.

The courses I have taught at UA or Tri-C include:

  • Math for Everyday Life / Contemporary Math
  • Intermediate Algebra
  • College Algebra
  • Precalculus
  • Intro to Discrete Math
  • Calculus with Business Applications
  • Calculus I–III
  • Differential Equations

I have also tutored students in a variety of other courses, including Linear Algebra, Applied Numerical Methods, and other topics ranging from basic arithmetic up through fourth-year undergraduate mathematics. Furthermore, I have occasionally tutored adjacent subjects, including computer science, statistics, physics, and chemistry. Those are outside my specialty, but I can usually be helpful at the introductory level.

Fees: My standard rate is $50/hour (minimum 1 hour), plus a per-session travel fee, if applicable. I offer a no-risk guarantee for the first hour: if it isn’t working out, we can stop there at no charge to you. (No one has yet exercised that option, but I continue to offer it for potential clients’ peace of mind.)

To calculate what travel fee may be necessary, I first use Google Maps to determine the distance of the round trip to our meeting location from my home or office, depending on where I will usually be departing from or returning to based on our schedule. (I live just south of Akron in the Portage Lakes area, and I work on the main UA campus.) I then multiply that distance by the IRS standard mileage rate for the current year. (For 2020, that’s 57.5¢/mile.) I round down to the nearest multiple of $5, then absorb the first $5 of that cost myself. Any excess, I must pass along to the client as a necessary charge to cover the cost of my transportation.

I realize that the description of my method may sound somewhat esoteric or cumbersome. When negotiating locations and schedules, I will calculate the relevant travel fees and share that information with you in advance, as I understand that it may impact your decisions. I have no desire to surprise you with hidden fees!

Location: With new clients, I usually conduct sessions in a public place, such as a library, café, or fast food restaurant. (Some students prefer quiet and reserve study rooms in libraries, while others like a bit of background noise or the option to get a bit to eat or drink. I have little preference myself.) In-home tutoring may also be an option once we all get to know each other a bit, or if we connect through a personal referral.

Payment methods: I accept cash, checks, and Google Pay.

Scheduling: Inquire for availability. I am generally pretty flexible with scheduling and rescheduling, within the bounds of my availability.
